
This project focuses on analyzing employee data to help organizations understand workforce trends, employee behavior, and key HR metrics.
The analysis helps HR teams make data-driven decisions related to retention, performance, and workforce planning.
The project uses real-world HR data to identify patterns in employee demographics, attrition, job roles, and compensation.

# Objectives

Analyse employee demographics and job profiles
Identify factors affecting employee attrition
Understand department-wise and role-wise employee distribution
Study salary trends and experience levels
Provide actionable insights for HR decision-making

# Dataset Description

The dataset contains employee-level HR information such as:
Employee ID
Age
Gender
Department
Job Role
Education
Years of Experience
Monthly Income / Salary
Attrition (Yes/No)
Performance Rating

(Dataset sourced from publicly available HR analytics data)

# Tools & Technologies Used

Power BI (for interactive dashboards)
Excel (initial data cleaning & exploration)

# Project Workflow

Data Collection
Imported HR employee dataset
Data Cleaning
Removed missing and duplicate values
Standardized categorical columns
Checked data consistency
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Employee distribution by department & role
Attrition analysis
Salary and experience trends
Gender and age analysis

#Visualization

Bar charts, pie charts, and trend graphs
Power BI dashboard for interactive analysis
Insights & Conclusions
Identified high-attrition departments
Observed salary and experience impact on retention

# Key Insights

Certain departments show higher attrition rates
Employees with lower experience and lower salary are more likely to leave
Work-life balance and job role play a significant role in retention
Senior employees tend to have lower attrition
